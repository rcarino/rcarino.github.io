It's sha boy Ray-Z in the building!

Raymond Arthur Carino (born 1990), known by his stage name Ray-Z, is a basketball phenom, weightlifter, and software artist. Ray-Z believes code is the essence of beauty. He adeptly blends minimalism, functional paradigms, and object oriented programming. The result is sheer elegance. Ray-Z's moniker is an homage to the rapper Jay-Z. Similar to how Jay pushed pills and counted bills, Ray pushes code and counts bills.

Software Engineering Career

2008-12: University

Mr Carino received his Bachelor's degree in computer science from UC Berkeley. He also tore up the basketball courts at the Recreational Sports Facility. Carino was initially a post-up player due to his towering height of 5'11.33" and imposing weight of 230lbs. He has since added a silky smooth jumper and aggressive dribble drive to his game after trimming his weight to 200lbs.

2012-14: First Job

Mr Carino worked the first 2 years of his career at a top media company. Prior to joining, the company's tech stack was a hodgepodge of acquired codebases with no cross-pollination between teams and projects. Mr Carino was instrumental in creating a modern content management system and front-end framework that bridged the code bases of several top traffic sites.

2014-present: Second Job

Mr Carino joined a pre IPO ad-tech company. The company has since IPO'd and is currently trading at more than twice its IPO price. Presumably, Mr Carino played at least a small part in this by shipping several high impact projects.
