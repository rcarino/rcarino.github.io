Raymond Carino

It's sha boy Ray-Z in the building!

Raymond Carino (born 1990) is better known by his stage name Ray-Z. Ray-Z is a basketball phenom, weightlifter, and software artiste. He adeptly blends minimalism, functional paradigms, and object oriented programming. The result is sheer elegance. Unbeknownst to many Ray-Z's moniker is actually an homage to the rapper Jay-Z. Similar to how Jay pushed pills and counted bills, Ray pushes code and counts bills.

Software Engineering Career

2008-12

Carino received his Bachelor's degree in computer science from UC Berkeley. While there, he garnered a reputation as a pick-up basketball phenom. Carino gained renown as a post-up player due to his imposing stature of 5'11.33", 230lbs. He has since added a silky smooth jumper and aggressive dribble drive after trimming to 200lbs. He frequently draws comparisons to Lebron James.

2012-14

Carino worked the first 2 years of his career at a top media company. Prior to joining, the company's tech stack was a hodgepodge of acquired codebases with no cross-pollination between teams and projects. Mr Carino was instrumental in creating a modern content management system and front-end framework that bridged the code bases of several top traffic sites.

2014-2015

Carino joined a pre IPO ad-tech company. The company has since IPO'd and is currently trading at more than twice its IPO price. Presumably, Mr Carino played at least a small part in this by shipping several high impact projects.

2015-2016

Carino signed with an enterprise backup-solution company as an early frontend engineer (Series A). He contributed to many core features which have since elevated the company to a $1B+ valuation. Alas, the partnership would not last. The commute was untenable and Carino signed closer to home during free agency (1 year cliff).

2016-Present

Carino accepted a deal with a botique fitness booking company. He is currently expanding his arsenal by adding hardcore backend skills. More concretely, he is quickly becoming an expert in the following buzzwords: Docker, Postgres, Microservices, functional programming via Java 8 / Python 3, React, etc.
